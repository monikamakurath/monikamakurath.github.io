My name is Monika. I am currently a postdoctoral researcher at HHMI Janelia where I study carbohydrate metabolism in cells. I grew up in a minority culture of Kashubia whose people live one with nature, and I've been captivated by trying to understand how nature works ever since. I am also a first generation college student and a first generation American. The generational patterns of addiction, poverty, and mental disorders have closely affected my family, and as a result they have strongly impacted my thinking. I deeply care about people and nature, and I try to understand both through my research. I got B.A. at Loyola University and Ph.D. at University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign. My Ph.D. research focused on studying the biophysical mechanisms of DNA unwinding using single-molecule approaches. During my postdoc and in my future research, I choose to focus on metabolism because it is fundamental to all life and it is tightly connected to behavior, diet, and diseases and predispositions affected by lifestyle choices.
