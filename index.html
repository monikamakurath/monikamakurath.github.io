  <h1>Research Interest </h1>

  <p>Metabolism is a paradoxical force—both the architect of life and, when imbalanced, a quiet saboteur—yet in its complexity lies an ancient beauty: a universal code that powers not only cells, but our choices, health, and the capacity to adapt and evolve. I am interested in uncovering the rules of metabolism to understand our choices, our health, and the fundamental logic that sustains all life on Earth. 

<p>

 <h2>Biography</h2>

<p>

My name is Monika, and I am a postdoctoral fellow working in the lab of Jennifer Lippincott-Schwartz at HHMI Janelia where I image metabolism in cells with fluorescence microscopy. I got my PhD in the lab of Yann Chemla at the University of Illinois of Urbana-Champaign where I studied molecular motors using optical traps. I am an alumna of Physical Biology of the Cell course in Woods Hole taught by Rob Phillips, Julie Theriot, Jane Kondev, and Hernan Garcia—a course which strongly sharpened my biophysics’ eye and shaped my philosophy and attitude towards scientific research.
