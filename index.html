Hi. My name is Monika. I am a postdoctoral researcher at HHMI Janelia where I study carbohydrate metabolism in cells. I grew up in a minority culture of Kashubia whose people live closely with nature, and I've been captivated by how nature works ever since.

I am also a first-generation college student and a first-generation American. The generational patterns of addiction, poverty, and mental illness have closely affected my family, and strongly impacted my thinking. As a result, I care deeply about both people and nature, and I try to understand both through my research.

I earned my B.A. from Loyola University in Chicago and my Ph.D. from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, where I studied biophysical mechanisms of DNA unwinding using single-molecule approaches. During my postdoc and in my future research, I focus on metabolism because it is fundamental to all life and it is tightly connected to behavior, diet, and the ways our evironments and choices influence our health.
